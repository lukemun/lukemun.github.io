Adventure BioFeedback Speech Pathology App

This web app was built to help speech pathologists and their patients. Since COVID-19 has made in person doctors visit impossible, speech pathologists must have their sessions over Zoom. This presents several problems as audio and video over Zoom is compressed and laggy. This application allows speech pathologists to accurately analyze patients voices while abiding by Personal Health Information laws.

I was the project owner for this application and lead a team of 2 engineers. I planned the software architecture, deliverables with our stakeholder, and demoed the app to speech pathologists. We utilized an agile development cycle and finished the application during Fall 2020 semester.

The two photos above demonstrate two of the features of the application. The first is pitch detection using a tensorflow library. The second is Formant detection which extracts vowel sounds from voice.

I built another board a year later. This time I researched and experimented with different designs. I ordered custom built parts and hacked a Wii Nunchuk to control the board. The end product was a capable of 42mph.

This project introduced me to engineering. I got exposed to Mechanical, Electrical, and Computer engineering. As the project progressed and I went from working with mechanical parts to coding translations inputs from my Wii Nunchuck into signals for my speed controller.
